About Yam Porridge

Yam porridge/Potage is one of the Nigerian delicacies that I love so much with an amazing taste. This yam porridge/ potage recipe is absolutely delicious especially when you carefully choose the right ingredients for a rich and better taste.

Most people prefer to have their yam porridge/ Potage garnished with some fresh leafy greens like spinach, scent leaves, and Kale for more nutritional value and taste while some people prefer to have it plain. I love the former so much because of its richness but whichever way you choose; with or without are both yummy.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Use African yam for authentic texture and flavor
  • Don't overcook the yam - it should be soft but still hold shape
  • Add palm oil for traditional color and richness
  • Fresh palm oil gives the best flavor and color

Instructions

1

Prepare the Yam

Peel and cut the yam into medium-sized chunks about 2 inches thick. Ensure that you rinse the yam more than once until the water is clear and no longer cloudy. The yam should be in medium-sized chunks about 2 inches thick.

Tip: Keep yam pieces uniform for even cooking.
2

Prepare the Pepper Mix

Blend your bell pepper, tomato, and half onion. Blend the bell pepper, habanero, and half onion. Set these aside. This will make a perfect pepper mix for the porridge.

Tip: The blend of these three will make a perfect pepper mix.
3

Cook the Yam

Cook the yam and the pepper mix together. Put the first yams inside a pot, and add the pepper mix, sliced onions, Knorr cubes, and water. Cover and let it cook for 10 minutes on medium to high heat.

4

Add Palm Oil

Add the fresh palm oil and leave to cook for 10 minutes on medium to high heat. This helps to cook out the raw taste of the palm oil and gives the dish its characteristic color.

Important: Fresh palm oil gives the best flavor and color.
5

Add Seasonings

Add the palm oil, ground crayfish, and salt to taste. Cover again and cook for another 10 minutes. The yam should be getting soft at this point.

6

Mash the Yams

You can use a potato masher or a wooden spoon. I love to mash some yams and keep some chunks. It gives more texture! Add your preferred fish or any other protein.

Texture tip: Partial mashing gives the perfect consistency.
7

Add Protein

Add the fish. You can smoke fish, dried, boiled, canned, or stockfish. You can also add smoked turkey or any other meat you prefer. Add the ground crayfish and cook for 3-5 minutes.

8

Add Vegetables

Stir in the herbs or any other leafy vegetables of your choice. Simmer for an additional 3 to 5 minutes. Serve and enjoy while still hot!

Final tip: Don't overcook vegetables to maintain nutrients and vibrant color.

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently with a little water or stock to restore consistency. The flavors actually improve the next day!

Add beans for extra protein, or try with sweet potatoes instead of yam. Some people add a little smoked paprika for extra depth. You can also make it spicier with more peppers.

For extra richness, add a tablespoon of butter or coconut oil at the end. If it becomes too thick, add hot water gradually while stirring.

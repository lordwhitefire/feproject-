About Garri

Garri, otherwise known as Cassava Flakes, is one of the main sources of carbohydrates and a popular staple food in Nigeria. It is a local delicacy eaten across Nigeria, usually mixed with sugar, milk, etc and eaten however you desire.

It is a fine to coarse granular flour of varying texture made from cassava tubers (the root of the cassava plant), which are cleaned after harvesting and grated. The water and starch are squeezed out and it is left to ferment. It is then fried and eaten in various ways.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Use Ijebu garri for drinking - it has the finest texture and best flavor
  • Always sieve garri before use to remove any dirt or lumps
  • Use cold water for drinking garri - hot water makes it too thick
  • For eba, use boiling water for the right consistency

Instructions

1

Sieve the Garri

Pour the garri into a bowl and add cold water gradually. Use a fine sieve to remove any dirt or large particles. This first wash is very important as you want to have clean garri before drinking it.

Tip: Use far more water than garri so that any dirt pours out with it.
2

Wash and Settle

Pour the water out of the garri, removing the dirt. Then add a small amount of water to the garri and stir. The garri should be in a bowl, not a plate, to prevent spillage.

Note: You can use cold water for this step. How much you use depends on the consistency you're looking for.
3

Add Sweeteners

Finally add your ingredients of choice and stir to combine well. Enjoy your garri. You can prepare garri plain or with sugar or salt, or both together.

Classic combo: Sugar + milk + groundnuts = perfection!
1

Boil Water

Bring water to a rolling boil in a kettle or pot. The water should be very hot for proper eba formation.

2

Add Garri to Bowl

Put the garri in a clean bowl. Create a well in the center for adding water.

3

Add Hot Water

Pour the boiling water into the garri gradually while stirring with a wooden spatula or turning stick. Mix until you achieve a smooth, stretchy consistency.

Key: Add water gradually to avoid making it too soft.
4

Mix and Shape

Continue mixing until smooth and lump-free. If too soft, add more garri; if too hard, add a little hot water. Shape into a ball or serve directly.

Pro tip: Cover with a warm cloth to keep soft while serving.

Recipe Notes

üçΩÔ∏è Serving Suggestions

Drinking garri: Enjoy as a refreshing drink or light meal. Eba: Serve with any Nigerian soup - egusi, ogbono, okra, or vegetable soup. Perfect for scooping up delicious soups!

‚ùÑÔ∏è Storage

Store garri in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. It has a long shelf life (6-12 months) when stored properly. Keep away from moisture to prevent mold.

üîÑ Variations

Try different types: Ijebu (finest), white, yellow (palm oil fortified). Mix with coconut water instead of plain water. Add cocoa powder for chocolate garri drink!

‚ö° Quick Tip

For drinking garri, the consistency should be pourable but not too watery. For eba, it should be firm enough to hold its shape when scooped.

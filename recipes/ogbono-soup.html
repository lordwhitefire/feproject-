About Ogbono Soup

Ogbono soup, also known as draw soup, is a beloved Nigerian dish made from the ground seeds of the wild African mango tree (Irvingia gabonensis). The seeds create a unique mucilaginous texture that makes the soup "draw" or stretch, hence the nickname.

Beyond its distinctive texture, ogbono soup is packed with nutritional benefits including healthy fats, protein, and fiber. It's traditionally served with swallow foods like pounded yam, fufu, or garri, allowing you to scoop up every delicious drop of this nutritious soup.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook ogbono - excessive heat can reduce its thickening properties
  • Add ogbono to hot oil, not water, for best results
  • The soup thickens as it cools, so adjust consistency accordingly
  • Mix ogbono with a little oil before adding to prevent lumps

Instructions

1

Prepare the Ogbono

If using whole ogbono seeds, grind them thoroughly using a dry mill or coffee grinder. Sift the powder to remove any large particles. Mix the ground ogbono with a small amount of palm oil to form a smooth paste.

Tip: Mixing with oil prevents lumps when adding to the soup.
2

Prepare Proteins

Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, one stock cube, and half of the chopped onion. Add water to cover and cook for 30-40 minutes until tender. Add the dry fish and stock fish in the last 10 minutes of cooking.

Tip: Cook tougher meats first, then add softer ones.
3

Prepare Vegetables

Wash and slice the leafy vegetables. If using ugwu (pumpkin leaves), remove the tough stems and slice thinly. For spinach, simply wash and drain. Set aside.

4

Heat the Oil

Heat the palm oil in a clean dry pot until hot but not smoking. You can add minced onions at this stage for extra flavor (optional).

5

Add the Ogbono

Remove the pot from heat and add the ogbono-oil mixture. Stir continuously with a wooden spoon to dissolve the ogbono completely. Return to low heat and add a little water or meat stock, then leave to boil for a minute. You'll notice it has become thicker and has a slimy texture.

Key: The ogbono should start to "draw" or stretch when properly dissolved.
6

Add Meat and Seasonings

Add more of the meat stock or water and leave to boil for 10 minutes. Stir constantly to prevent burning. Add the boiled meat, dry fish, stock fish, stock cubes, ground crayfish, pepper, and salt to taste.

7

Final Seasoning

Mix well, cover and leave to simmer for about 10 minutes. You should notice the change in the aroma of the soup. Stir and add the shredded spinach or ugwu leaves.

8

Add Vegetables

Simmer for 1-2 minutes and turn off the heat. Now your ogbono soup is ready. The vegetables should retain their vibrant color and slight crunch.

Final tip: Don't overcook the vegetables to maintain nutrients and texture.

Recipe Notes

üçΩÔ∏è Serving Suggestions

Serve hot with any Nigerian swallow: eba, fufu, pounded yam, or semovita. The draw texture makes it perfect for scooping. Also delicious with rice or boiled yam.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Storage

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The soup will thicken when cold. Reheat gently with a little water to restore consistency. Can be frozen for up to 2 months.

üîÑ Variations

Add bitterleaf for a more traditional taste. Some regions add okra for extra draw. You can also add uziza seeds or leaves for a peppery kick. Ogbono-egusi combo is popular too!

‚ö° Quick Tip

If your ogbono soup becomes too thick, add hot water gradually while stirring. If too thin, mix a small amount of ogbono with oil and add to the soup.

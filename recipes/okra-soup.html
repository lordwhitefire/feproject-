About Okra Soup

Nigerian Okra soup is simply a stewed okra soup recipe. It is a very popular delicacy among the Yoruba ethnic group of western Nigeria and is known for its quick preparation time and nutritional benefits. The soup gets its characteristic viscous texture from the okra, making it perfect for pairing with swallow foods.

Just like ogbono soup and Ewedu soup, Okra soup has a viscous texture which makes it an acquired taste for some, but it's incredibly nutritious and delicious when prepared properly. The combination of fresh okra, assorted proteins, and traditional seasonings creates a comforting and satisfying meal.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Use fresh, tender okra for best results - older okra can be tough and fibrous
  • Chop or blend okra just before cooking to prevent browning
  • Don't overcook the okra - it should retain some texture
  • Add vegetables at the end to preserve nutrients and color

Instructions

1

Prepare the Okra

Wash the okra thoroughly. You can either chop them finely with a knife or blend 1/3 of the okra and chop the rest. If you want it chunky with no elasticity, chop all or blend all if you don't fancy any chunkiness. Clean the smoked fish and soak in hot water to remove all traces of dirt, shred and set aside.

Tip: I prefer mine both ways - some blended for texture and some chopped for crunch.
2

Boil the Meats

Boil the meats on medium to high heat with the chopped onion, Knorr cubes, garlic, and salt to taste. Start with tougher meats like shaki, cow foot, ponmo, add softer meats as you go. If you're using stock fish, add with the softer meats.

Tip: Season your meats really well - if your stock isn't well seasoned, your soup may end up tasting bland.
3

Prepare Seasonings

Slice the fresh peppers, grind the pepper and set aside. Ensure you end up with very rich and tasty stock. You'll need at least 5 cups of stock left in the pot after boiling the meats.

4

Start the Soup Base

When the meats are soft, transfer them into a bowl, leaving just the stock in the pot. Add the palm oil and leave to boil on medium heat for 6-8 minutes.

Important: This step helps to cook out the raw taste of palm oil.
5

Add Seasonings

Add the ground pepper, then the locust beans (if using). Leave to cook for 3 minutes. Now add the ground crayfish, then the chopped bell peppers.

6

Add the Okra

Leave to cook for 3 minutes. Now add the blended okra. Stir vigorously to combine with the meat stock. Add the chopped okra and stir.

Key: Stir well to achieve the perfect draw texture.
7

Add Proteins

Add the shredded smoked fish, smoked prawns, cooked meats and smoked fish back into the pot. Stir and combine then add the chopped okra.

8

Final Cooking

Add the fresh prawns if you're using any, leave to cook for 5 minutes. And it's ready. Serve with any swallow of your choice, I love mine with white or yellow garri (eba).

Final tip: Don't overcook the prawns - they become rubbery if overdone.

Recipe Notes

üçΩÔ∏è Serving Suggestions

Serve with white garri (eba), semovita, or pounded yam. The viscous texture makes it perfect for scooping. Also delicious with boiled rice or yam for a lighter meal.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Storage

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. The okra may continue to release its mucilage, making it thicker. Reheat gently with a little water if needed.

üîÑ Variations

Add ogbono for extra draw, or mix with egusi for a combination soup. Some people add a little baking soda to enhance the green color. You can also add uziza leaves for extra flavor.

‚ö° Quick Tip

For extra green color, add a pinch of baking soda to the okra while cooking. To reduce the sliminess, add a little lemon juice or vinegar.

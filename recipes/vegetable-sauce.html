About Vegetable Sauce

The Nigerian vegetable sauce is what I think came from someone who maybe wanted to make use of leftover vegetables (especially people that cook in large quantities. Things like salad and so, tends to bring about some left over) or wanted to create a food that specializes more on vegetables.

In Nigeria especially, most food do not focus heavily on vegetables unlike countries like Korea etc. In fact as we speak, most vegetarians in Nigeria are as a result of health problems or old age though there are pure vegetarians, they are just too few as Nigeria is a purely non-vegetarian country.

üí° Pro Tips

  • Use a variety of colorful vegetables for visual appeal and nutrition
  • Don't overcook vegetables - they should retain some crunch
  • Add vegetables in order of cooking time (harder ones first)
  • Irish potato puree makes the perfect thickener

Instructions

1

Prepare the Chicken

Wash and cut the whole chicken into pieces. Place in a pot, add chopped spring onions, stock cubes, and thyme. Add water to cover the contents of the pot and start cooking.

Tip: Use a whole hen for richer flavor than broiler chicken.
2

Season the Stock

When done, add salt to taste and top up the water to the same level as the contents if necessary. This creates a rich, flavorful base for your sauce.

3

Prepare the Vegetables

Wash and cut the vegetables: plum tomatoes (remember to remove the seeds), carrots, cabbage, and bell pepper as shown. Remember to scrape the carrots before cutting. Set these aside.

Prep tip: Keep vegetables separate as they cook at different rates.
4

Make the Thickener

Peel the Irish potatoes, wash and cut into small pieces. Blend into a thick paste and set aside. This will be used as thickener for the Vegetable Sauce.

Thickener tip: The smoothest puree usually settles at the bottom - use only that part.
5

Cook the Chicken

On medium heat, add the chopped tomatoes and the vegetable oil. Cover and cook for 15 minutes. Once it boils, add the chopped tomatoes and vegetable oil.

6

Add Carrots

Add the carrots and cook for 5 minutes. Carrots take longer to cook, so they go in first.

7

Add Remaining Vegetables

Now, add the potato pur√©e, chopped bell peppers, and cabbage. Stir, cover, and allow to cook for 5 minutes on high heat. Add salt to taste and the sauce is ready to be served.

Final step: Don't overcook cabbage - it should retain some crunch.

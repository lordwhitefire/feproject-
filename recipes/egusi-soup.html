About Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a staple in Nigerian cuisine, made from ground melon seeds (egusi) that give it a distinctive nutty flavor and thick consistency. This nutritious soup is packed with protein from the melon seeds and can be customized with your favorite vegetables and proteins.

Traditionally served with pounded yam, fufu, or garri, egusi soup brings families together around the dining table. The combination of ground melon seeds, palm oil, and fresh vegetables creates a symphony of flavors that represents the heart of West African cooking.

  • Use a mix of meats (beef, goat, tripe) for richer taste
  • Add spinach at the very end to maintain its vibrant color

Instructions

1

Prepare the Egusi

Grind the egusi seeds into a fine powder using a dry mill or coffee grinder. If you bought pre-ground egusi, sift it to remove any lumps. Set aside in a bowl.

Tip: Lightly toast the egusi seeds before grinding for enhanced flavor.
2

Prepare the Meat

Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, one stock cube, and half of the chopped onion. Add water to cover the meat and bring to a boil. Cook for 30-40 minutes until tender. Reserve the stock.

Tip: Cook tougher meats first, then add softer ones later.
3

Prepare the Base

Heat palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the remaining chopped onion and saut√© until translucent. Add the ground peppers and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently.

4

Add the Egusi

Add the ground egusi to the pot and stir continuously for 5 minutes. The egusi will absorb the oil and form a paste. Gradually add meat stock while stirring to prevent lumps.

Tip: Add stock gradually to control the consistency.
5

Add Meat and Seasonings

Add the cooked meat, smoked fish (if using), ground crayfish, and remaining stock cube. Stir well and let it simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

6

Add Vegetables

Add the fresh spinach and stir gently. Cook for 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are wilted but still vibrant green. Adjust salt to taste.

Tip: Don't overcook the vegetables to maintain nutrients and color.
7

Final Simmer

Reduce heat to low and let the soup simmer for another 5 minutes. The soup should be thick but pourable. Add more stock or water if needed to achieve desired consistency.

Recipe Notes

Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, garri (eba), or semovita. The traditional way is to scoop the soup with the swallow, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Store leftover soup in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat gently, adding a little water or stock to thin if necessary.

üîÑ Variations

Try adding bitterleaf instead of spinach for a more traditional taste. You can also add ugwu (pumpkin leaves) for extra nutrition and authentic flavor.

‚ö° Quick Tip

The soup thickens as it cools, so always make it slightly thinner than your desired consistency. Reheat with a splash of water or stock.

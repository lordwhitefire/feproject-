About Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is more than just a dish‚Äîit's a cultural phenomenon that sparks friendly debates across West Africa. This one-pot wonder combines rice with a rich tomato base, creating a symphony of flavors that represents the heart of African cuisine.

Known for its distinctive orange color and smoky flavor (especially when cooked over firewood), Nigerian jollof rice is the life of every party. The secret lies in the perfect balance of tomatoes, peppers, and spices, slow-cooked to create that signature taste that keeps everyone coming back for more.

Instructions

1

Prepare the Rice

Wash the rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming sticky. Soak in cold water for 30 minutes, then drain completely.

Tip: Soaking helps the rice cook evenly and reduces cooking time.
2

Blend the Base

Wash and roughly chop the tomatoes, red bell peppers, and half of the onion. Blend until smooth. You should have about 4 cups of blended mixture.

Tip: For authentic flavor, use a 3:1 ratio of tomatoes to peppers.
3

Fry the Base

Heat vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add the remaining chopped onion and saut√© until translucent. Pour in the blended tomato-pepper mixture and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring frequently, until the sauce thickens and the oil separates.

Critical: Don't rush this step! Proper frying prevents sour taste.
4

Add Seasonings

Add the stock cubes, thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, and salt to taste. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.

5

Add Stock and Rice

Add the chicken stock and bring to a boil. Add the drained rice and stir gently to combine. The liquid should be about 1 inch above the rice level. Add more stock or water if needed.

Tip: Use a wooden spoon to avoid breaking rice grains.
6

Cook the Rice

Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Reduce heat to low and cook for 20-25 minutes. Resist the urge to stir! Check after 20 minutes - if the rice is tender and liquid absorbed, it's done. If not, add a little more stock and continue cooking.

Secret: Wrap the lid with foil to create a tighter seal for perfect steam circulation.
7

Steam and Fluff

Once the rice is cooked and liquid absorbed, turn off the heat. Let it steam for 5 minutes with the lid on. Remove the lid, add the mixed vegetables if using, and fluff gently with a fork to separate the grains.

Pro tip: For party jollof, let the bottom layer caramelize slightly for that signature smoky flavor.

Serve with fried plantains, coleslaw, and your choice of protein (chicken, beef, or fish). Jollof rice is also delicious with moi-moi or salad. Don't forget the chilled drink!

‚ùÑÔ∏è Storage

Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Reheat with a splash of water or stock to restore moisture. Jollof rice also freezes well for up to 3 months.

üîÑ Variations

Try coconut jollof by replacing some stock with coconut milk. Add shrimp or diced chicken for protein. For vegetarian version, use vegetable stock and add more vegetables.

‚ö° Quick Tip

The secret to authentic party jollof is the smoky flavor from slightly burning the bottom layer. Don't be afraid of a little caramelization!
